Understanding the Risks

Here's a complete breakdown of the risks involved with any activities you may do or infomation you share online. Stay safe out there!

Activites Done Online

Activity Risk Level Points Why it Might Be Risky
Lurking / reading Low 1 Passive activity; no data is shared.
Liking / reacting Low 1 Minimal data is shared; patterns may still be tracked.
Following accounts / subscribing Low 1 May reveal interests, but limited direct exposure.
Sharing links to external content Medium 2 Can indicate views and interests; can include tracking links.
Commenting Medium 2 Reveals opinions; may include personal info unintentionally.
Posting content Medium 2 May contain identifying details or patterns of behaviour.
Messaging (private chats/DMs) Medium 2 Private, but still stored and potentially exposed.
Creating/joining public groups/events Medium 2 Shows interests, affiliations, or physical presence.
Participating in forums Medium 2 Often permanent, searchable, and potentially identifying.
Uploading videos High 3 Can reveal appearance, voice, surroundings, identity.
Livestreaming High 3 Real-time exposure with high potential for oversharing.
Syncing contacts High 3 Shares your personal network with the platform.
Using location-based apps High 3 Tracks movements and habits in real time.
Tagging people or places in posts High 3 Reveals identity, location, and relationships.

Information Shared Online

Type of Info Shared Sensitivity Level Points Why it Might Be Risky
Username / nickname Low 1 Often not linked to real identity, but can be reused across platforms.
First name only Low 1 May be common, but contributes to identification when combined with other data.
Email address Medium 2 Can be used for tracking, spam, or phishing attempts.
School / workplace Medium 2 Gives away daily location and age group or profession.
Date of birth Medium 2 Common for security questions; reveals age.
Opinions or beliefs (e.g. politics) Medium 2 May be used to profile or target you with content or ads.
Phone number High 3 Used for verification, but also enables spam, scams, and tracking.
Home address High 3 Reveals where you live; risk of stalking or physical harm.
Browsing history (via linked accounts) High 3 Can build an in-depth profile of your habits and interests.
Third-party app permissions High 3 Allows other apps access to your data, often with little transparency.
Photos with faces High 3 Facial recognition and identity exposure risks.
Videos with voice/image High 3 Reveals multiple personal characteristics and details.
Real-time location Very High 4 Can be used to track movements and current position.
Identity documents (ID/passport) Very High 4 Highly sensitive; can lead to identity theft or fraud.
